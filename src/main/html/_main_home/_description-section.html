Description

This is a very exciting geography game. At the start, it puts the player in a random location of Google Street View (a service of panoramic photos). The player can "walk" around the location to see more references. The main goal is to guess where the player was placed: exact continent, exact country, state, city/town, etc...

You have been given a 2D-map, that you can move/zoom. You have to mark your guess and click "Guess". You will be shown the actual location, guess accuracy and other details.

You don't have to spend hours and hours looking for the right answer. It takes about 2 minutes: start, look for a couple of references, mark your guess and click "Guess". That's all! Many players do this way. But it's not very interesting and doesn't make much progress. You can dive deeper and spend lots of time to find lots of references and guess much more accurate. I think it's wonderful!

But nobody forbids you to go professional way, like me. You can use the Internet. Yes, you can: translate foreign texts, look for the streets and shops that you see, use sandboxes, etc. In short, you can do everything with this game. It's the most interesting way playing this game.

The "couple-minute" method will give you much more points, than you would get if you try the "deep" method. But the second method is much more interesting. It's your choice: more points, but not interesting, or fewer points, but more interesting. Yes, points also depend on time. Shown below in the Tutorial.

You can do everything with this game, except "hacking". All the data (including the game) is loaded into your browser's cache and storage, so you can replace some sources, call a "winning" function to automatically win the game, use the built-in devtools to see the current location, etc. It's not even called "hacking", but "cheating". Game-cheating is not against the law, but it's not fair. Nobody forbids doing this, but it's not interesting. So do anything, except hacking. If you really want to hack the game - do it only for education/experience in programming. If you want to enjoy the gameplay - do it without hacking. But don't forget: real hacking is against the law (when you harm other users, break the service, etc.).
